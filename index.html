Sin

and He said to them, “Thus it is written, that the Christ would suffer and rise again from the dead the third day, and that repentance for forgiveness of sins would be proclaimed in His name to all the nations, beginning from Jerusalem. Then He opened their minds to understand the Scriptures, You are witnesses of these things.

Luke 24:45-48

I would say that we all are sinners. The Bible verses above speak about how the Christ would suffer and rise again from the dead the third day, ... so that repentance for forgiveness of sins could be proclaimed in His name to all the nations. So sin is indeed sin. None is good save God. God is Good, and we are evil. It is easy to recognise our condition as wrongdoers. Our conscience testifies that we're guilty and rightly so.

Repentance

From that time Jesus began to preach and say, “Repent, for the kingdom of heaven is at hand.”

Matthew 4:17

Once we've discovered the seriousness of our crimes, of our deeds, it is common for a God-fearing heart to long for repentance. I would humbly suggest that we need to be in this stage, honest with ourselves, honest with God. Did we truly saw the sin we've committed or are we just being courteous ? As all the secrets of men's hearts are laid bare before the Creator, we need to be sincere in our repentance for Him to be able to receive and cleanse us otherwise we could soothe our hearts and conscience with emotions and afterward go back to our sins.

Savior Christ Jesus

And He said to them, “O foolish men and slow of heart to believe in all that the prophets have spoken! Was it not necessary for the Christ to suffer these things and to enter into His glory?” Then beginning with Moses and with all the prophets, He explained to them the things concerning Himself in all the Scriptures.

Luke 24:25-27

The Savior talks about Himself in the Scriptures. We need therefore, if we want to listen to Him to read His words which are found in the Bible. He is Love. He is our Wisdom, He is our Savior who died on account of our sins. Him, God has chosen to be sacrificed for us. He is our Lord. He is God. He is both the Son of God and the Son of man. He is Holy. When we seek God, we encounter Him, for He is the Mediator between man and God. As it is better to read His words for ourselves than to listen to me or someone else, you should get a good Bible translation to start reading His words.

New Birth

Jesus answered, “Truly, truly, I say to you, unless one is born of water and the Spirit he cannot enter into the kingdom of God. That which is born of the flesh is flesh, and that which is born of the Spirit is spirit. Do not be amazed that I said to you, ‘You must be born again.’ The wind blows where it wishes and you hear the sound of it, but do not know where it comes from and where it is going; so is everyone who is born of the Spirit.”

John 3:5-8

God says we can't see His kingdom if we are not born again. It is a must as it is made clearer to Nicodemus. In our first birth we inherit the vain manner of life from our parents. In our spiritual birth however, we renounce to Satan and his angels, the world with all its pomp, whom we're serving in the past, to become slaves unto righteousness. The Word of God regenerates us into children of God. From there on, we are His children and He is our Father.

The Kingdom of God

“But when the Son of Man comes in His glory, and all the angels with Him, then He will sit on His glorious throne. All the nations will be gathered before Him; and He will separate them from one another, as the shepherd separates the sheep from the goats; and He will put the sheep on His right, and the goats on the left. “Then the King will say to those on His right, ‘Come, you who are blessed of My Father, inherit the kingdom prepared for you from the foundation of the world. For I was hungry, and you gave Me something to eat; I was thirsty, and you gave Me something to drink; I was a stranger, and you invited Me in; naked, and you clothed Me; I was sick, and you visited Me; I was in prison, and you came to Me.’ Then the righteous will answer Him, ‘Lord, when did we see You hungry, and feed You, or thirsty, and give You something to drink? And when did we see You a stranger, and invite You in, or naked, and clothe You? When did we see You sick, or in prison, and come to You?’ The King will answer and say to them, ‘Truly I say to you, to the extent that you did it to one of these brothers of Mine, even the least of them, you did it to Me.’

Matthew 25:31-40

“Then He will also say to those on His left, ‘Depart from Me, accursed ones, into the eternal fire which has been prepared for the devil and his angels; for I was hungry, and you gave Me nothing to eat; I was thirsty, and you gave Me nothing to drink; I was a stranger, and you did not invite Me in; naked, and you did not clothe Me; sick, and in prison, and you did not visit Me.’ Then they themselves also will answer, ‘Lord, when did we see You hungry, or thirsty, or a stranger, or naked, or sick, or in prison, and did not take care of You?’ Then He will answer them, ‘Truly I say to you, to the extent that you did not do it to one of the least of these, you did not do it to Me.’ These will go away into eternal punishment, but the righteous into eternal life.”

Matthew 25:41-46
